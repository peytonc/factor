Square-Free Modular Orbit Decomposition:
The proof relies on the ring isomorphism provided by the CRT for coprime moduli. The CRT establishes a bijection ψ : ℤ/Mℤ → ∏ (ℤ/p_iℤ). The state transition matrices G ∈ {A​, B​, C​} contain only integer entries. Therefore, for any state vector v, the operation Gv (mod M) is equivalent to applying G component-wise to the vector residues modulo each p_i as (Gv) (mod M) ⟷ ((Gv) mod(p_1), (Gv) mod(p_2), ..., (Gv) mod(p_n)). The group generated by {A​, B​, C​} acts transitively on the valid quadric surface modulo each prime p_i (Theorem 6).
While the matrix generators form a fiber product of index 2 within ∏ PGL(2, ℤ/p_iℤ) due to a shared ℤ/2ℤ determinant quotient dictated by the global sequence of B transitions, their determinant-1 subgroups map to the projectivized special linear groups PSL(2, ℤ/p_iℤ). These subgroups share no common non-trivial quotients, meaning that by Goursat’s Lemma, their joint action is fully surjective across the product of the individual prime orbits. Because each PSL(2, ℤ/p_iℤ) acts transitively on its respective modular quadric orbit O_(p_i), this global group-level constraint does not restrict coordinate reachability; the spatial orbit O_M remains a completely unconstrained Cartesian product ∏ O_(p_i).
