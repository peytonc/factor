# T09 — Square-Free Modular Orbit Decomposition (Proof)

> Proof of [T09 — Square-Free Modular Orbit Decomposition](../theorems/T09-squarefree-orbit-decomposition.md).

## Argument

The proof relies on the ring isomorphism provided by the CRT for coprime moduli. The CRT establishes a bijection `psi : Z/MZ -> prod (Z/p_iZ)`. The state transition matrices G in {A, B, C} contain only integer entries. Therefore, for any state vector v, the operation `Gv (mod M)` is equivalent to applying G component-wise to the vector residues modulo each p_i: `(Gv) mod M  <->  ((Gv) mod p_1, ..., (Gv) mod p_n)`. The group generated by {A, B, C} acts transitively on the valid quadric surface modulo each prime p_i (Theorem 6).

While the matrix generators form a fiber product of index 2 within `prod PGL(2, Z/p_iZ)` due to a shared Z/2Z determinant quotient dictated by the global sequence of B transitions, their determinant-1 subgroups map to the projectivized special linear groups PSL(2, Z/p_iZ). These subgroups share no common non-trivial quotients, meaning that by Goursat's Lemma, their joint action is fully surjective across the product of the individual prime orbits. Because each PSL(2, Z/p_iZ) acts transitively on its respective modular quadric orbit O_(p_i), this global group-level constraint does not restrict coordinate reachability; the spatial orbit O_M remains a completely unconstrained Cartesian product `prod O_(p_i)`.
