Generalized Modular Orbit Decomposition:
Definitions:
    Let M be any odd integer with the prime factorization M = p_1^k_1 * p_2^k_2 * ... * p_n^k_n, where p_i are distinct primes and k_i ≥ 1.
    Let Q_i = p_i^k_i be the maximal prime power component of the modulus.
    Let O_M = Orbit(M) be the set of reachable states in the PPT graph modulo M.
    Let O_(Q_i) = Orbit(Q_i) be the set of reachable states in the PPT graph modulo the prime power Q_i.
    Let Φ : O_M → O_(Q_1) × O_(Q_2) × ... × O_(Q_n) be the canonical projection map defined by v ↦ (v mod(Q_1), v mod(Q_2), ..., v mod(Q_n)).
    O_M ≅ ∏ O_(Q_i) for i=1..n, be the generalized modular orbit decomposition.
    |O_(Q_i)| = p_i^(2*(k_i - 1)) * |O_(p_i)| is the cardinality of the prime power orbit (derived via Hensel lifting of non-singular points).
    |O_M| = ∏ |O_(Q_i)| for i=1..n, be the generalized modular orbit cardinality.
Description:
    Generalizes the decomposition of any odd integer M by treating maximal prime powers (p^k) as atomic moduli. While the orbit modulo a composite M splits into orthogonal components, the orbit modulo a prime power p^k does not split; instead, it is a "lifted" structure of the base prime orbit. The global orbit is isomorphic to the Cartesian product of these prime power orbits.
